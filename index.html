Here’s a product I conceived and created using Google AI Studio, within the first two weeks of my AI Tools class. SnapBit BeReal, but for the habits that change your life After completing 75 Hard in the summer of 2025, I wanted an app that captured what that challenge felt like — the daily accountability, the photo proof, the community of people pushing each other. So within the first two weeks of my AI Tools class at Tulane, I built it. SnapBit is a social habit-tracking app where you set a goal, snap a photo every day as proof, and stay accountable with friends no matter where they are in the world. AI monitors every comment to block negative or discouraging language, keeping the community purely supportive. And when you submit a photo, AI analyzes it against your stated goal — if it doesn’t align, it gets rejected. No cheating, no shortcuts. Coalition to Restore Coastal Louisiana (CRCL) Louisiana Gulf Coast Restoring the Louisiana coastline, one oyster shell at a time Joined a coalition of volunteers to physically restore Louisiana’s rapidly eroding coastline. We collected discarded oyster shells from seafood restaurants across New Orleans and Baton Rouge — shells that would otherwise go to landfill — and used them to rebuild reef structures that protect the shoreline and restore marine habitat. Louisiana loses a football field of coastal land every 100 minutes. This work made that statistic real and personal. Studying coastal policy in the classroom and then standing on that coast with a shovel in your hand are two very different things — and both matter.
